# Instructor Guide — Two-Round MVP

## Intended learning outcomes

Students should be able to:

1. convert an ambiguous hiring request into job-related outcomes, deliverables and criteria;
2. challenge preference-based or pedigree-based requirements;
3. develop a weighted competency scorecard with observable indicators;
4. design a balanced sourcing portfolio under budget constraints;
5. write an inclusive job advertisement, usable Boolean search and personalised outreach message;
6. explain the trade-offs among reach, quality, speed, cost and inclusion.

## Hidden logic overview

### Round 1: 100 points

- Intake-question quality: 20
- Hiring brief: 40
- Competency scorecard: 40

The strongest intake questions elicit business outcomes, deliverables, success measures, stakeholders, constraints and day-one capabilities. Preference, competitor and school-pedigree questions consume limited meeting capacity without materially improving the role analysis.

The hiring brief is checked for business and customer outcomes, concrete deliverables, evidence-based essential criteria, genuinely desirable criteria, employment conditions and measurable success indicators. Small penalties apply to age-coded language, school pedigree, native-language restrictions and unnecessarily rigid retail tenure.

The competency scorecard rewards role-relevant competencies, defensible weights and observable behavioural indicators. “Years of retail experience” is intentionally included as a trap: it is background evidence, not a competency.

### Round 2: 100 points

- Channel portfolio: 35
- Job advertisement: 30
- Boolean string: 15
- Outreach message: 20

A strong channel portfolio normally combines active and passive sourcing, internal and external reach, and targeted and broad channels. There is no single mandatory combination. Heavy reliance on an agency is deliberately inefficient within the fictional budget.

The advert should communicate the transformation, outcomes, responsibilities, evidence requirements, conditions, inclusion and process. The Boolean search should use title synonyms, domain terms and proper grouping without demographic filters. The outreach message should be tailored to Camille's actual profile and offer a low-pressure conversation.

## Debrief themes

- Job analysis versus reproduction of the manager's initial preferences
- Essential versus desirable criteria
- Competency versus experience or credential
- Internal mobility and fairness
- Active versus passive talent pools
- Reach versus relevance
- Inclusive attraction language
- Boolean precision versus over-restriction
- Candidate value proposition and transparency
- Limits of rule-based and AI-generated feedback

## Pilot observation sheet

During testing, note:

- fields students misunderstand;
- expected good answers that the rubric fails to recognise;
- weak answers that score too highly;
- whether a dominant channel combination emerges;
- completion time by stage;
- technical friction;
- quality of the debrief discussion.

Revise `data/scoring_rubrics.json` after each pilot rather than adding more interface complexity immediately.
